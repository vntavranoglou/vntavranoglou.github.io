AI Governance Risk Assessment — ChatGPT Use in Companies

1. Overview

Many companies are starting to use AI tools like ChatGPT to improve efficiency. However, the use of AI introduces legal, operational, and reputational risks that must be managed to maintain professional standards and regulatory compliance.

2. Key Risks

  • Hallucinations: AI may generate incorrect or misleading information that looks highly plausible, requiring expert verification.
  • Data Leakage: Risk of employees inputting confidential company data or trade secrets into public AI models, potentially compromising intellectual property.
  • Accountability: Complexities in determining responsibility for AI-generated outputs in a legal or commercial context.

3. Legal & Regulatory Considerations

  • Data Protection: Risks of GDPR breaches if personal data is processed via third-party LLMs without adequate safeguards.
  • Intellectual Property: Uncertainties regarding the ownership of AI-generated content and potential infringement of existing copyrights.
  • Professional Negligence: The danger of relying on AI-generated legal citations or advice without human-in-the-loop oversight.

4. Recommended Mitigation Measures

  • Human-in-the-loop: Mandating a senior human review of all AI-generated work product before it is finalized.
  • Input Policies: Implementing strict "no-confidential-data" rules for public AI tools.
  • Staff Training: Specialized training on prompt engineering and hallucination detection.
  • Internal Governance: Developing a clear AI Usage Policy (AUP) tailored to the specific firm’s risk profile.

5. Conclusion

AI tools like ChatGPT offer significant advantages for legal operations, but they are not a replacement for professional judgment. A robust governance framework is essential to leverage these tools safely and effectively.
